# Derivatives Evidence

## Use When

Use for China government-bond futures, interest-rate swaps, basis, IRR, CTD, DV01, hedging, curve trading, and interest-rate options.

## Source Reports

- `cicc-interest-rate-derivatives-2025`
- `huatai-bond-market-framework-2025`

## Core Framework Claims

- Derivatives connect cash-bond curve exposure, funding cost, futures or swap pricing, basis, IRR, DV01, hedge objective, and liquidity.
- A hedge decision and a directional trade require different risk budgets, data, and evaluation metrics.
- Derivatives can reduce or transform exposure, but add margin, basis, roll, liquidity, model, counterparty, and funding risks.

## Data Fields Mentioned

- Futures price, contract, deliverable basket, CTD, conversion factor, basis, IRR, implied repo, open interest, liquidity.
- Cash-bond curve, cheapest deliverable candidates, repo eligibility, duration, key-rate DV01.
- Swap curve, fixing rate, floating-leg convention, collateral, counterparty rules, hedge ratio, margin.

## Common Misreadings

- Mixing a hedge objective with a directional trade recommendation.
- Making a basis-trade conclusion without CTD, conversion factor, IRR, repo, and liquidity.
- Ignoring margin and funding risk when using futures or swaps.
- Treating DV01 hedge as eliminating basis and curve-shape risk.
