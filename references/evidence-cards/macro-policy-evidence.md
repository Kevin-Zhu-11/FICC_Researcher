# Macro Policy Evidence

## Use When

Use for macro data releases, expectation-gap analysis, policy reaction, social financing, money supply, inflation, PMI, fiscal policy, and macro-to-rates transmission.

## Core Framework Claims

- Macro analysis should identify the economic "temperature" and "season" instead of reading one indicator mechanically.
- The market trades expectation gaps: actual data versus priced-in expectation, not actual data alone.
- China's macro structure requires separating demand, supply, income distribution, credit, fiscal support, and policy reaction.
- Policy is endogenous: weak data can trigger easing, but FX, bank NIM, financial stability, and bond supply can constrain timing and form.
- Supply-side policies such as anti-involution may affect prices and profits without creating broad demand recovery.
- The analyst should classify indicators by timing layer: leading, coincident, and lagging.
- China's macro map should track economic circulation, not only the old three-demand components.
- For bonds, fiscal policy has a dual role: growth support and duration supply.
- For low-rate environments, institution behavior and liability stability can dominate simple macro beta.

## Standard Analysis Map

```text
1. Data and source
2. Indicator timing layer
3. Demand / supply / price / policy / market-pricing map
4. Economic circulation affected
5. Expectation gap and previous value
6. Policy objective and policy tool
7. Funding, supply, curve, credit, and institution-behavior transmission
8. Missing data, risks, and counterexamples
```

## Common Misreadings

- Treating social financing strength as private demand strength without decomposing government bonds and loan structure.
- Treating weak macro data as automatically bullish for bonds without policy and supply checks.
- Treating CPI/PPI moves as demand recovery without checking supply and base effects.
- Treating policy wording as action without timing, tool, and constraint analysis.
- Treating one-month data as a trend without checking seasonality and cumulative-series arithmetic.
- Treating M2 strength as transaction activity without checking M1 and deposits.
- Treating government-bond financing as the same signal as household and corporate medium-long loans.

## Output Guardrails

- If a single-month value is derived from cumulative values, show the formula and rounding caveat.
- If market expectations are unavailable, state that the surprise cannot be measured.
- If current yield, spread, NCD, repo, or fund-flow data is unavailable, limit the conclusion to directional transmission.
- If WebSearch is blocked or only snippets are available, treat it as a lead and prefer official or connector data.
