## Core Framework Claims

- Credit spreads are driven by default risk, liquidity compensation, investor structure, supply, and leverage convenience.
- In low-spread environments, return enhancement depends on selection, timing, coupon carry, curve positioning, and limited credit sinking; do not rely only on spread compression.
- City investment and financial-credit bonds require specialized issuer and instrument analysis beyond generic rating buckets.

## Data Fields Mentioned

- Spread by rating, sector, region, tenor, issuer type, and liquidity bucket.
- Issuance, cancellation, maturity wall, refinancing access, rating changes, default or extension events.
- Region fiscal capacity, debt ratio, land-sale revenue, policy support.
- Bank capital ratios, TLAC, subordinated terms, coupon deferral, write-down or conversion clauses.

## Common Misreadings

- Using rating alone as credit quality.
- Treating low spread as safety rather than possible crowding.
- Treating policy support as full guarantee.
- Ignoring liquidity and exit cost when credit sinking.
