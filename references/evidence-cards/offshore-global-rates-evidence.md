# Offshore Global Rates Evidence

## Use When

Use for US Treasuries, global rates, Chinese USD bonds, dim sum bonds, offshore RMB bonds, FX constraints, hedge cost, and cross-border funding.

## Source Reports

- `cicc-us-treasuries-framework-2025`
- `cicc-offshore-credit-usd-dim-sum-2025`
- `cicc-offshore-rmb-bonds-2025`

## Core Framework Claims

- Offshore debt analysis separates global risk-free rates, Fed path, FX and hedge cost, issuer credit, legal terms, and liquidity.
- Onshore credit conclusions cannot be transferred directly to offshore instruments because offshore bonds add currency, legal ranking, cross-border policy, rating, and liquidity layers.
- Cross-border relative value requires hedge-cost and funding substitution analysis before comparing yields.

## Data Fields Mentioned

- UST curve, Fed path, real yields, term premium, DXY, USD/CNH, hedge cost, cross-currency basis.
- Issuer spread, rating, guarantee or standby letter of credit, legal ranking, maturity, liquidity.
- Onshore comparison curve, onshore funding alternatives, offshore issuance, investor base.

## Common Misreadings

- Applying onshore implicit-support assumptions directly to offshore debt.
- Comparing onshore and offshore yield without FX hedge cost.
- Treating UST moves as one-for-one domestic long-end signals.
- Ignoring offshore legal terms, liquidity, and stale spread quotations.
