# Institution Wealth Evidence

## Use When

Use for bank, insurance, wealth-management, mutual-fund, broker, foreign-investor, pension, and redemption-feedback questions.

## Source Reports

- `huatai-institution-behavior-2025`
- `cicc-low-rate-investor-behavior-2025`

## Core Framework Claims

- Institution behavior translates financing demand, policy, and liability conditions into marginal bond demand.
- Net-value wealth products and mutual funds transmit market volatility through NAV drawdown, redemption, liquidity management, and forced selling.
- Liability stability, accounting, regulation, tax, leverage, and investment scope determine each institution's behavior.

## Data Fields Mentioned

- Bank deposits, NCD issuance, loan demand, interbank assets, liquidity ratios.
- Insurance premium, liability duration, solvency, long-bond allocation.
- Wealth product NAV, shares, redemption flow, duration, leverage, cash buffer, rating distribution.
- Mutual fund shares, duration, leverage, holdings, performance, subscription/redemption.

## Common Misreadings

- Treating all institutions as yield-chasing buyers.
- Assuming redemption pressure always sells the riskiest asset first.
- Inferring holdings only from product return.
- Ignoring accounting category and liquidity terms.
