# Policy Reaction Function

Use this file when a macro signal requires a policy-response judgment.

## Core Idea

Policy is not an external label. In China macro-to-rates analysis, weak growth, low prices, FX pressure, financial stability, fiscal supply, and asset-price expectations can change the policy reaction itself.

Do not stop at:

```text
data weak -> yields down
data strong -> yields up
```

Instead use:

```text
data signal
-> policy objective affected
-> likely policy tool and timing
-> funding, supply, expectation, and risk-appetite channel
-> yield-curve impact
```

Use the Huatai "policy, fundamentals, sentiment" triad:

```text
policy wind
-> fundamental flag
-> market sentiment and positioning
```

When the three move in different directions, the answer must explain the conflict rather than force a one-line conclusion.

## Four Objectives

- Growth and employment.
- Price stability.
- Financial stability.
- Balance of payments and FX stability.

## China Policy Map

For domestic rates work, classify policy into:

- Monetary policy: price, quantity, balance-sheet tools, and communication.
- Fiscal policy: budget deficit, government-bond supply, special bonds, fiscal spending pace, and quasi-fiscal tools.
- Property and consumption policy: demand stabilization, income expectation, property-chain repair.
- Industrial and supply-side policy: capacity discipline, anti-involution, technology investment, supply-chain security.
- Market-stability policy: exchange rate, equity market, credit risk, local-debt resolution, bank NIM.

Policy can support growth and increase bond supply at the same time. Always split the growth channel from the supply channel.

## Policy Tool Map

### Monetary Policy

- Quantity: RRR, OMO, MLF, structural tools, relending, PSL.
- Price: policy-rate corridor, OMO/MLF/LPR adjustment, deposit-rate guidance.
- Communication: monetary policy report, central-bank meetings, official statements.
- Constraint: bank NIM, FX, financial stability, bond-market leverage, real-economy transmission.

### Fiscal Policy

- Deficit and budget expenditure.
- Local-government special bonds.
- Ultra-long special treasury bonds.
- Policy-bank and quasi-fiscal financing.
- Fiscal spending pace and fund allocation efficiency.

### Supply-Side And Demand-Side Policies

- Demand side: consumption support, income support, fiscal transfer, property stabilization.
- Supply side: capacity discipline, anti-involution, industrial policy, technology investment.
- Transmission depends on whether the economic circulation is smooth.

## Scenario Rules

### Weak Data

Do not jump directly to "rates bullish". Check:

- Is weakness from real private demand, government-bond timing, base effects, or data seasonality?
- Does it trigger credible monetary easing, fiscal acceleration, or only verbal support?
- Does fiscal acceleration create duration supply pressure?
- Are short-end funding rates already low enough that marginal easing has limited effect?

### Strong Data

Do not jump directly to "rates bearish". Check:

- Is strength from private demand or public-sector financing?
- Does it reduce easing expectations or simply confirm policy effectiveness?
- Are inflation and credit quality also improving?
- Has the market already priced the improvement?

### Price Rebound

Separate:

- Demand-driven inflation.
- Supply restriction or anti-involution-driven price rebound.
- Commodity or FX pass-through.
- Base effect.

Only demand-driven and policy-constraining price pressure should be treated as clearly rates-bearish.

## Bond-Market Translation

- Growth weak + policy easing credible: rates-friendly, but check fiscal supply.
- Growth weak + supply heavy + policy cautious: curve may steepen or stay volatile.
- Inflation weak from demand: rates-friendly.
- Inflation rebound from supply restriction: check whether it is price-only or demand-recovery driven.
- Fiscal expansion: growth support and bond supply pressure must be analyzed together.
- Stable FX requirement can delay price-based monetary easing.
- Local-debt resolution can reduce tail risk while also changing regional credit spreads and bank balance-sheet behavior.
